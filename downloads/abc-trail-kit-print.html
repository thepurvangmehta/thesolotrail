About this trek

The Annapurna Base Camp trek is the rare Himalayan route where the destination lives up to the reputation. You arrive inside a natural amphitheatre — Annapurna I (8,091m), Machhapuchhre (6,993m), and Hiunchuli (6,441m) rising on three sides. The approach through Gurung villages, rhododendron forests, and the Modi Khola gorge is as good as the summit.

No mandatory guide required in 2025/26. The trail is extremely well-marked and busy with other trekkers. The GPX file in this kit covers all major junctions where wrong turns happen.

💡
Best seasons Oct–Nov (crisp, clear, busy) and Mar–May (rhododendrons, good visibility). Avoid Jun–Sep (monsoon). Dec–Feb is possible but cold and requires extra gear.

Getting there

From India: Fly to Kathmandu (Tribhuvan International Airport). Take an early morning Yeti/Shree airlines flight to Pokhara (25 min, ~$70–100 one-way). Book domestic flight in Pokhara season — sell out fast.

Kathmandu → Pokhara overland: Tourist bus (~$10, 6–7 hours) or private car (~$80, 5 hours). Scenic but long — consider flying.

Pokhara → Trailhead: Taxi/jeep to Nayapul (~₹800 / $10) or directly to Kande/Phedi (~$12–15). Most solo trekkers take the direct Kande option to save a day.

⚠️
Arrive Pokhara the day before you start. Don't do the full journey in one day. Spend one night, sort permits in the morning, start Day 1 fresh and rested.

10-day itinerary

Classic route via Ghorepani and Poon Hill. For the 7-day direct route via Chhomrong, contact us at hello@thesolotrail.com for an updated GPX route file.

Day 1
Pokhara → Nayapul → Birethanti → Tikhedhunga
~4 hours · +850m · Altitude: 1,480m
Take an early morning taxi to Nayapul (30 min). Collect TIMS check at Birethanti checkpoint — have permits ready. Gentle walk along the Modi Khola river to Tikhedhunga. The famous Ulleri stone staircase begins tomorrow, so rest tonight.
Day 2
Tikhedhunga → Ulleri → Ghorepani
~5 hours · +1,400m · Altitude: 2,874m
The hardest single-day climb on the route. Ulleri is 3,000+ stone steps — start early, take it slow. The reward is Ghorepani: teahouses with Dhaulagiri views, hot dal bhat, and your first taste of Himalayan altitude. Hydrate aggressively today.
Day 3
Poon Hill sunrise → Ghorepani → Tadapani
4:30am start for sunrise · 5–6 hours · Altitude: 2,630m
Wake at 4:30am for the 45-minute climb to Poon Hill (3,210m). Clear days give 180° views of Dhaulagiri, Annapurna South, and Machhapuchhre. After breakfast, descend through rhododendron forests to Tadapani. The rhododendrons are spectacular in March–April.
Day 4
Tadapani → Chomrong (Chhomrong)
~4 hours · Altitude: 2,170m
Steady descent through forest to the Modi Khola valley, then a sharp climb to Chhomrong — the last major village before the sanctuary. Stock up: buy snacks, top up cash, charge devices. There is very limited phone signal after this point.
Day 5
Chhomrong → Sinuwa → Bamboo
~4 hours · Altitude: 2,310m
Steep descent from Chhomrong, then long climb through dense bamboo forest. This section is beautiful but humid — more like a jungle than the alpine trek you imagined. No signal; the GPX file in this kit covers the Sinuwa/Bamboo junction where trekkers occasionally miss the turn.

Day 6
Bamboo → Dovan → Himalaya Hotel
~4 hours · Altitude: 2,900m
Enter the Annapurna Sanctuary proper. The gorge narrows dramatically. The Himalaya Hotel area (3 teahouses) is your last comfortable rest before the altitude zone. If you feel any AMS symptoms, stop here — do not push to Deurali tonight.
Day 7
Himalaya Hotel → Deurali → MBC
~4 hours · Altitude: 3,700m
The Himalaya–Deurali section passes through an active avalanche corridor — move early (leave by 6:30am), move quickly, and do not linger. After Deurali the landscape opens to moraine and you enter the high-altitude zone. Drink water constantly. MBC at 3,700m.
Day 8 — Summit day
MBC → Annapurna Base Camp (4,130m)
~3 hours · Altitude: 4,130m · Maximum altitude
Leave MBC by 6am. The final approach reveals the full amphitheatre. ABC sits in a glacial bowl surrounded by eight peaks over 6,000m. Spend 2–3 hours here — morning is clearest before clouds build. Check your SpO2 at the teahouse. If below 80%, begin descent immediately.
🔴
AMS Warning — 4,130m At this altitude, headache, nausea, and light-headedness are common. These are manageable. Loss of coordination, confusion, or severe headache that won't respond to ibuprofen means descend immediately — do not sleep at this altitude if these symptoms appear.
Days 9–10
ABC → MBC → Chhomrong → Nayapul → Pokhara
Return route · ~6 hours/day
Descent is fast — most trekkers return to Pokhara in 2 days. Day 9: descend to Chhomrong. Day 10: push through to Nayapul and taxi back to Pokhara. Your knees will remind you about the Ulleri staircase on the way down. Trekking poles are non-negotiable on descent.

Packing essentials

Target total pack weight: under 10kg. Leave city clothes in your Pokhara hotel (most offer free storage).

Clothing & footwear

  • Down jacket (600-fill+)
  • Fleece mid-layer
  • Waterproof rain jacket + trousers
  • 3× moisture-wicking base layers
  • 2× trekking trousers
  • Warm hat + sun hat
  • Gloves (liner + waterproof shell)
  • Wool trekking socks ×4 pairs
  • Trekking boots (waterproof, ankle)
  • Camp sandals for evenings
  • Trekking poles (strongly recommend)

Documents & money

  • Passport + 2 passport photos
  • Travel insurance docs (printout)
  • NPR 40,000+ cash
  • Nepal SIM (Ncell — airport or Pokhara)

Gear & safety

  • Headlamp + spare batteries
  • Sleeping bag liner
  • Water bottles ×2 (1L) + purification tabs
  • Power bank (20,000mAh min.)
  • Sunscreen SPF 50+ + lip balm
  • Sunglasses (UV protection)
  • Universal adapter
  • Pulse oximeter (recommended)

First aid & meds

  • Diamox (consult doctor first)
  • Ibuprofen + Paracetamol
  • Blister kit (Compeed)
  • Rehydration sachets (ORS)
  • Antidiarrheal (Imodium)
  • Basic first aid kit
